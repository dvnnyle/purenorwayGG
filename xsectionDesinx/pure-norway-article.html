Why aluminium is
the only choice.

We didn't choose aluminium by accident. Here's the thinking behind the can — and why it matters more than you think.

DS
David Severinsen
CEO, PURENorway Water
Share

When we set out to build PURENorway, the question of packaging wasn't an afterthought — it was one of the first decisions we made. And it shaped everything that followed. The can is not just a container. It's a statement.

We looked at plastic. We looked at glass. We even considered cartons. But none of them held up to the values we'd built the brand around — purity, responsibility, and lasting impact. Then we landed on aluminium, and the answer became obvious.

Infinitely recyclable,
infinitely responsible.

Aluminium is one of the only materials on earth that can be recycled endlessly without losing any of its quality. A can you recycle today could be back on a shelf within 60 days. No degradation. No loss. Just the same material, in a new form, doing its job again.

Plastic, by contrast, degrades with every cycle — and most of it never gets recycled at all. Glass is heavy, fragile, and energy-intensive to transport. Aluminium is lightweight, strong, and chills faster than almost any other material. It keeps our water tasting exactly as it should: clean, crisp, and pure.

Zero carbon in
production.

We don't just say we care about the environment. Our entire production process is designed around a zero carbon footprint. That means the energy used, the water used, the materials used — all of it is accounted for and offset where it can't yet be eliminated.

It's not a perfect system. No production is. But it's a genuine commitment, and one we hold ourselves to with every batch we produce.

"The can you hold is the lightest possible version of itself — and the most responsible one we could make."

— David Severinsen, CEO

We're proud of the can. We're proud of what it represents. And we're committed to pushing further — exploring new ways to reduce our footprint, improve our supply chain, and give back more than we take. That's what I CARE means to us. Not a slogan. A standard.
